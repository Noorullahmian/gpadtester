How To Test Your Controller

Testing your gamepad is simple and takes less than a minute:

  1. Connect your controller via USB or Bluetooth (for wireless controllers)
  2. Press any button to wake up the controller if it's in sleep mode
  3. Navigate to our tester page and allow controller access when prompted
  4. Interact with all controller components - buttons, triggers, joysticks, D-pad
  5. Review the results and identify any potential issues

Understanding Your Test Results

Our tool provides color-coded feedback to help you quickly assess your controller's condition:

  • Green indicators show properly functioning inputs
  • Yellow indicators suggest minor issues that may need attention
  • Red indicators reveal significant problems requiring repair or replacement

For joysticks, we display the exact position and movement range, making it easy to spot drift or dead zones. Trigger inputs show the full range of motion from 0% to 100% depression.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why isn't my controller being detected?

First, ensure your controller is properly connected to your device. For wired controllers, try a different USB port. For wireless controllers, make sure they're properly paired. Some older controllers may require additional drivers. Also check that you've allowed gamepad access when prompted by your browser.

Can I use this tool on mobile devices?

Yes, our tester works on mobile devices that support game controllers. Many modern Android devices and iOS devices (running iOS 14.5+) support Bluetooth controllers. Connect your controller via Bluetooth and access our site through Safari (iOS) or Chrome (Android).

How accurate are the test results?

Our tool provides highly accurate results for button presses and joystick movements. The input latency measurements are accurate to within ±5ms under normal conditions. For professional-grade testing, we recommend using our tool in a controlled environment with minimal other USB/BT devices connected.

What should I do if my controller has stick drift?

For minor drift, many controllers have calibration options in their system settings. For more severe drift, you may need to clean the joystick mechanism with compressed air or isopropyl alcohol. Some controllers allow joystick module replacement. If under warranty, consider contacting the manufacturer.

Does this tool work with flight sticks and racing wheels?

Yes, many specialized controllers like flight sticks and racing wheels will work with our tester, though some advanced features may not be fully represented. These devices typically appear as game controllers to your system and can be tested for basic functionality.
